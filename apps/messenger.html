Features

Peer-to-Peer Messaging

Messages go directly between you and the recipient — no middleman server.

WebRTC Encrypted

End-to-end encryption powered by WebRTC ensures only you and your contact can read messages.

Messages Stay on Device

Your conversation history lives only on your devices — never stored on a server.

No Server Stores Messages

The server is used only for signaling (finding peers) — it never sees your messages.

Simple Clean UI

A minimal, distraction-free interface focused entirely on your conversations.

Free Account for Signaling

A free Firebase account is needed only to discover and connect with other users.

How It Works

Messenger uses Firebase Authentication for user identity and WebRTC for peer-to-peer connections. When you send a message, it travels directly to the recipient's device over an encrypted WebRTC data channel. The server only facilitates the initial connection — it never stores or reads your messages.

Your message history is stored locally on your device only.

Privacy

Messenger is designed for maximum privacy. Messages are end-to-end encrypted and never touch a server. Firebase Auth is used only for user identity and signaling. No message content is ever stored, logged, or accessible to anyone but the sender and recipient.

Open Source

Messenger is fully open source under the MIT license. You can inspect the code, verify the encryption, report bugs, or contribute improvements.
